About Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park, established in 1946, holds the distinction of being Kenya's first national park. Nestled just 7 kilometers (4 mi) south of Nairobi, the bustling capital city, it stands as a haven for wildlife amid the urban sprawl. Here, the savannah meets the cityscape, creating a unique juxtaposition of wilderness and civilization.

Spanning over 117 square kilometers, the park boasts diverse ecosystems, including open grass plains, woodlands, and riverine forests. Its varied landscapes provide sanctuary to a rich array of wildlife, from majestic lions and graceful giraffes to elusive cheetahs and endangered rhinos.

Visitors to Nairobi National Park can embark on unforgettable safari experiences, witnessing the iconic Big Five and marveling at the breathtaking scenery. The park also offers educational opportunities, with informative guided tours and interactive exhibits shedding light on conservation efforts and the importance of preserving Kenya's natural heritage.

Whether you're a nature enthusiast, a wildlife photographer, or simply seeking a tranquil escape from the city, Nairobi National Park invites you to immerse yourself in the wonders of Africa's wilderness, right on the doorstep of Kenya's vibrant capital.

Species Spotlight

Lion

The lion is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera.

Giraffe

The giraffe is an African artiodactyl mammal, the tallest living terrestrial animal, and the largest ruminant.

Elephant

The elephant is the largest existing land animal. It has a thick, long trunk and large ears.

Rhino

The rhinoceros, often abbreviated as rhino, is a member of the family Rhinocerotidae.

Zebra

Zebras are African equines with distinctive black-and-white striped coats.

Cheetah

The cheetah is a large cat native to Africa and parts of Iran. It is the fastest land animal.
